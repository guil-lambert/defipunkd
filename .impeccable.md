# DefiBeat — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

DeFi power users and researchers. They already understand proxies, multisigs, timelocks, role-based admin, upgrade paths, and onchain governance. They are not here to be sold to or onboarded. They arrive looking for specific evidence about a specific protocol — who controls it, what can be changed, by whom, with what delay — and they want that evidence dense, cited, and fast to scan.

Primary context of use: long research sessions on desktop, often with many tabs open, comparing protocols against each other or against onchain reality. Secondary: a reviewer / auditor pulling up a protocol page to cross-check a claim.

The job to be done: **find and verify a specific control-surface or provenance fact about a protocol, quickly, without being slowed down by marketing.**

### Brand Personality

Three words: **raw, dense, evidence-first.**

Voice: unbothered. Displays findings; does not perform for the reader. Closer to a lab report, a court exhibit, or a service manual than to a product. No charm offensive. No "let's make DeFi approachable." The reader is assumed competent.

Emotional goal: the quiet confidence of a primary source. The reader should feel they are looking at the data itself, not at a presentation of it.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Radiographic.** An x-ray view of protocol internals — clinical, unflinching, monochrome with surgical accents.

**Theme:** dark. Derived from the actual viewing context (long research sessions, dense tables, power-user desktop use), not chosen for coolness.

**Palette (OKLCH-convert from these hex seeds; tint neutrals toward the blue-gray brand hue):**

- `#08090C` — deepest ink, page base
- `#10131A` — surface / table background
- `#1E2330` — raised surface / row hover / divider reference
- `#D8E4EC` — primary text (softened white, never pure)
- `#6B7785` — secondary text, metadata, timestamps, units
- `#7BB4CC` — cool accent: links, active state, selection (sparingly)
- `#5A9178` — positive / verified / onchain-confirmed (sparingly)
- `#D13B3B` — negative / contradiction / missing-critical (sparingly)

Accents are evidentiary, not decorative. `#7BB4CC`, `#5A9178`, `#D13B3B` mean something specific every time they appear. If an accent is not carrying information, it should not be on the page. 60-30-10 by weight, heavy on the neutrals.

**Typography:** one-typeface utilitarian, carried hard. Primary recommendation: **ABC Diatype + ABC Diatype Mono** (Dinamo). Fallback if licensing is a blocker: **IBM Plex Sans + IBM Plex Mono** — acknowledged as a training-data reflex, but acceptable here because the clinical/technical brief is exactly what Plex was designed for and the user explicitly endorsed it. No display pairing. One quiet family doing all the work, sized and weighted for hierarchy rather than mixed with a "character" face.

**References (feel, not imitation):**
- L2BEAT's dense tables and risk-matrix layout (inherited directly from the fork)
- Bloomberg terminal's confidence that data is the subject
- Medical imaging viewers — Osirix, Horos — where chrome disappears and the scan is the interface
- Working-paper PDFs from NBER, BIS, CFR — information-dense, monochrome, footnote-heavy

**Anti-references — explicitly NOT:**
- DeFi-bro neon, glow, or "cyberpunk" accents
- Vercel/Linear template dashboards with gradient hero and rounded-2xl cards
- Crypto-exchange slick (animated number tickers, confetti, celebratory color)
- Friendly-illustration empty states or mascot-driven tone
- Any attempt to make evidence feel "fun" — the data is already interesting to the reader who came here

The failure mode to avoid is **trying too hard to be pleasant.** Neon for excitement, gradients for premium feel, hover micro-interactions for craftedness, warm palettes for approachability — all fail in the same direction. DefiBeat should read as if its authors consider UX charm slightly beneath the task.

### Design Principles

1. **Data is the subject.** Chrome recedes; borders, backgrounds, and type all defer to the content. If a decorative element is competing with a number, remove it.
2. **Accents are evidentiary.** Blue, green, red each carry a single meaning (link/active, verified-onchain, contradiction/missing). They never appear as decoration. Their rarity is the source of their power.
3. **One type family, hierarchy from weight and size.** No display fonts. No "personality" pairings. Mono for addresses, hashes, tabular numerals, and any value a reader might copy.
4. **Density over breathing room.** Unlike marketing sites, this interface rewards packing information. Tight leading in tables. Small-but-readable metadata. Generous space is reserved for separating *sections*, not for decorating rows.
5. **Unbothered, not unfriendly.** Plain labels. No exclamation marks. No "Oops!" empty states. When something is missing, say it is missing and cite the reason; do not apologize or soften.
6. **Every claim has provenance visible.** Design must accommodate inline citations, commit SHAs, timestamps, and source links without those becoming visual noise — they belong in `#6B7785` secondary type, mono where appropriate, adjacent to the claim they support.
